Hand-carved rubber stamps · Ink on paper
About the Artist

Marta Vidal is a printmaker and illustrator based in Porto, Portugal. She works primarily in hand-carved rubber stamps — cutting the image in reverse into a block of soft rubber, inking it, and pressing it onto paper by hand. Each impression is slightly different. The ink bleeds where the pressure is uneven. The edges aren't clean. That's the point.

Before this commission, she had never listened to the Grateful Dead. We sent her the four collections and told her to listen for a week before starting. She came back with a question: "Why do all of these sound like they were recorded in a cathedral?" She spent the next month carving.

The Process

Each collection got its own stamp. Marta carved a single block for each — the speakers for Six Hundred Four, the elephants for New Animal, the recording equipment for Young Bettys, the librarian's desk for Cornell and Company. She printed dozens of impressions, varying the ink weight and pressure, before selecting the final versions.

The stamps were printed on uncoated stock in a single color — a dark indigo ink on warm off-white paper. No digital manipulation. The texture you see in the artwork is the actual texture of ink pressed into paper by hand.
