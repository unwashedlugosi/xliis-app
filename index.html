Every three months, XLIIs features four Grateful Dead tape collections from four different collectors around the world. Each one spent years — sometimes decades — tracking down the highest-quality recordings and organizing them around a single idea.

We photograph their original cassettes and J-card inserts. We commission an artist to create new artwork for the collections. Then we put the tapes on loan for a three-month run.

These are this season's collectors.

The Artist

Each season, XLIIs commissions one artist to create artwork for all four collections. The artist chooses the medium. The collections share a visual language — but only for that season.

Spring 2026's artist worked in stamps. Hand-carved, ink on paper. Each collection got its own stamped illustration — speakers and amplifiers for 1974, circus elephants for Brent's era, a librarian's desk for 1977, Betty's recording equipment for the early years.

Next season will be a different artist, a different medium. We don't know what it will look like yet.
