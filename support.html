XLIIs — Support

Getting Started

Pick a collection. Choose a show. Press play. Your position saves automatically, so you can close the app and come back to exactly where you left off.

FAQ

Is it free?

Completely. No ads, no subscriptions, no in-app purchases.

Where does the music come from?

All recordings are streamed from the Internet Archive, where the Grateful Dead community has shared soundboard recordings for decades with the band's blessing.

Why can't I hear anything?

Make sure your phone isn't on silent mode and the volume is up. The app streams audio, so you need an internet connection.

What are the collections?

Cornell and Company (1977), Wall of Sound (1974), Young Bettys (1969-1973), and New Animal (1979-1980). Each collection is a curated set of soundboard recordings from a specific era. The collections rotate every three months.

What does "XLIIs" mean?

XLII is the Type II high-bias cassette tape that tapers used to record and trade live shows. The name is a nod to those tapes and the culture of collecting and sharing them.
